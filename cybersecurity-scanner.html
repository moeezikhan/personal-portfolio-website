General security tips
🔒
Always Check for HTTPS
Look for the padlock icon and "https://" in the URL. HTTP sites don't encrypt your data, making it easy for attackers to intercept.
🔍
Inspect the Domain Carefully
Phishing sites often use lookalike domains like "paypa1.com" or "amazon-secure.net". Always check the root domain before entering credentials.
⚠️
Beware of Shortened URLs
Short links (bit.ly, tinyurl) hide the real destination. Use a URL expander before clicking links from unknown sources.
🛡️
Use a Password Manager
Password managers autofill only on the correct domain, making them an automatic defense against phishing sites that mimic real ones.
📧
Don't Trust Email Links
Hover over links before clicking. Legitimate companies rarely ask you to click an email link to verify your account or reset a password under urgency.
🔄
Keep Software Updated
Most attacks exploit known vulnerabilities. Keeping your browser, OS, and apps updated closes the majority of common attack vectors.
📱
Enable Two-Factor Authentication
Even if your password is stolen, 2FA (especially hardware keys or authenticator apps) prevents attackers from accessing your accounts.
🌐
Use a VPN on Public Wi-Fi
Public networks are easy to intercept. A VPN encrypts your traffic, preventing man-in-the-middle attacks on cafes, airports, and hotels.
